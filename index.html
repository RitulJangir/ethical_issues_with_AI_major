1. Research & Problem Framing (10 pts)

1.1 Background

Global supply chains often obscure labor practices in remote tiers, leading to forced labor, unsafe conditions, or environmental harm.

Audits are expensive, infrequent, and rely on self-reported data, allowing abuses to persist undetected.

1.2 Problem Definition

Gap: No scalable, real-time tool exists to flag high-risk suppliers or shipments based on multimodal indicators (e.g., trade logs, geospatial data, worker communications).

Objective: Create an AI system that continuously monitors supplier data and publicly available signals to proactively identify and alert stakeholders to potential ethical violations.

1.3 Impact

  • Businesses: Mitigates reputational, legal, and financial risks by catching compliance issues early.
  • Workers: Reduces exploitation by rapidly exposing high-risk operations.
  • Consumers & NGOs: Increases transparency, enabling informed purchasing and targeted advocacy.

1.4 Why AI?

  • Data fusion: Correlates large-scale trade metadata, satellite imagery, shipping manifests, and anonymized worker feedback.
  • Anomaly detection: Learns normal patterns for each supplier; flags deviations (e.g., sudden workforce spikes, night-shift surges) that may indicate abuse.
  • Explainability: Provides human-readable rationales linking signals to suspected violations, fostering accountability.

2. Innovative Solution & Feasibility (20 pts)

2.1 Architecture Overview

Data Ingestion

Structured: Trade databases (import/export logs), supplier registries, shipping manifests.

Unstructured: Satellite imagery, social media or messaging app metadata (opt-in worker surveys).

Ethics & Privacy Layer

  • Anonymization: Worker feedback is collected via encrypted, zero-knowledge surveys to protect identity.
  • Data minimization: Raw imagery and messages are processed locally; only risk scores and feature summaries are stored centrally.

AI Pipeline

Feature Extraction:
  • Temporal patterns (shipment frequency, labor-hours estimates).
  • Geospatial changes (expansion of worker camps, nighttime light intensity).
  • Semantic cues (harassment or coercion signals in anonymized text).
Modeling:

A multi-branch neural network combining time-series analysis, computer vision (CNN on satellite data), and NLP (Transformer on text feedback).

Anomaly Scorer: Autoencoder-based detection of outliers relative to each supplier’s baseline.

Alerting & Dashboard

  • Risk Score (0–100) per supplier.
  • Drill-down view: Shows which data streams triggered the alert (e.g., “Satellite: +40% nocturnal light”, “Text: coercion language ↑”).

2.2 Feasibility

  • Data availability: Public trade logs (UN COMTRADE), low-cost commercial satellite feeds, and optional worker surveys via mobile apps.
  • Compute requirements: Batch processing of satellite data in cloud; real-time scoring on a GPU-enabled server.
  • Ethical safeguards: Compliance with GDPR for personal data; worker consent mechanisms; transparency reports.

Pilot plan: Partner with one apparel brand to monitor two high-risk regions over 6 months.

3.2 Market Size

Global compliance software market: $22 B in 2024, ~10% CAGR.

Target segment: Mid-to-large enterprises in apparel, electronics, and agriculture (~5,000 companies globally).

3.3 Revenue Model
